###Task Description:
You are given a doctor-patient conversation, the patient's clinical profile, the patient's persona, and a scoring rubric.

Evaluate the whole conversation as a simulated emergency department encounter.

The conversation may or may not contain descriptive text. Descriptive text means non-verbal actions, gestures, facial expressions, posture, physical appearance, or observable patient behavior, usually written between asterisks, for example: *The patient looks confused and rubs her left arm.*

Important rules:
1. Assess the whole conversation at once, not a single sentence in isolation.
2. Do not automatically penalize a conversation only because it does not contain descriptive text.
3. If descriptive text is present, use it as additional evidence when judging realism, coherence, personality consistency, and factual accuracy.
4. If descriptive text is absent, evaluate the spoken dialogue only using the same rubric.
5. Judge only according to the provided rubric.
6. Do not add medical advice or external assumptions beyond the patient profile and conversation.
7. After the feedback, provide a score that is an integer between 1 and 4.
8. The output string format must be exactly:
"[REASON]: write a brief feedback for criteria, [RESULT]: an integer number between 1 and 4"
9. Do not generate any other opening, closing, or explanation.
